Biotech Breakthrough: Biotech Genetics Experiment

Question: Is race"real" from a biological perspective

1. Claim

Race is not biologically real.

2.Evidence

In our class experiment. We collected our own DNA by using saliva and a toothpick, and then afterwards, when we experimented, We found out that students matched with people who were not even alike in physical appearances, could still match by the DNA code that we specifically took, proving that Race is not biologically real.

3. "Reasoning1 Part one

Biological race is an idea that humans can be divided into different groups based on phenotype, such as skin color, hair texture, or eye shape. Furthermore, people of the same race would be more genetically similar to each other than other people in other races.

Genetics is the study of DNA and inherited traits. Scientists can use DNA to compare people and determine how similar they are biologically. Looking at DNA helps answer whether race is biologically real.

4. Reasoning part two">

The evidence from our experiment does not support the idea of biological race. People with different skin colors sometimes shared similar genetic traits, and people with similar appearances often had different traits. This shows that physical appearance does not accurately predict a person's genetics.

If biological race were real, we would have observed people with the same skin color consistently sharing the same genetic traits and DNA patterns. However, the results showed that genetic variation exist among all groups of people. Therefore. race is not biologically real because human genetic differences do not fit into speperate racial categoris.
